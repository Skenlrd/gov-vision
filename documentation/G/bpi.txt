2. Mandatory Step: The Aggregation Phase (ETL)
Because BPIC 2017 is an "Event Log" (1.2M rows), you must aggregate it into a "Case Log" (31,509 rows) to match your MongoDB schema before any ML can occur. 
1.	Grouping: Group all events by the unique identifier case:concept:name. 
2.	Field Extraction:
o	createdAt: Take the min(time:timestamp) for each group. 
o	completedAt: Take the max(time:timestamp) for each group. 
o	status: Extract the name of the very last event in the sequence (e.g., "A_Denied", "A_Pending").
o	cycleTimeHours: Calculate the decimal hour difference between completedAt and createdAt. 
o	revisionCount: Count the number of "O_Create Offer" events in the group and subtract 1. 
o	rejectionCount: Count total occurrences of "O_Refused" or "A_Denied" activities. 
o	stageCount: Perform a simple count of all events (rows) belonging to that Case ID. 
o	departmentId: Map the org:resource of the first event. 
o	priority: Map the case:RequestedAmount into three bins: Low, Medium, High.

Following the "PDF Method" established in your progress report, this model identifies workflows that deviate from departmental standards.

2.	Scale: Apply StandardScaler. This is non-negotiable because cycleTimeHours (range 0–500) will otherwise overpower revisionCount (range 0–5).

4. AI Step 2: Predictive Delay Forecasting (Prophet)
This model identifies future performance trends for each department. 
•	Logic: Time-series decomposition into trend + weekly/yearly seasonality. 
•	Training Data Preparation:
1.	Aggregate the 31,509 cases by their createdAt date.
2.	Create a dataframe with ds (date) and y (Average cycleTimeHours for that day). 
•	Training Process (80%):
1.	Temporal Split: Use the first 80% of the timeline (Jan 2016 – Oct 2016). Do not shuffle. 
2.	Fit: Initialize Prophet() and call model.fit(). 
•	Testing (20%):
1.	Forecast the remaining 20% of the timeline (Nov 2016 – Feb 2017).
2.	Validation: Calculate RMSE (Root Mean Square Error) between the predicted yhat and the actual recorded hours in the test period.

5. AI Step 3: AI-Driven Risk Scoring (Random Forest)
This assigns a risk probability the moment a request enters the system. 
•	Logic: Supervised classification using an ensemble of decision trees. 
•	Target Labeling (Supervised):
1.	Create a column is_at_risk.
2.	Rule: If daysOverSLA > 0 OR status == 'denied', then is_at_risk = 1. Else, 0. 
•	Training Process (80%):
1.	Feature Selection: Use static features known at submission: departmentId, priority, hourOfDaySubmitted, and ApplicationType. 
2.	Encoding: Convert categorical strings into numeric values (One-Hot Encoding).
3.	Fit: Train a RandomForestClassifier on the 80% split. 
•	Testing (20%):
1.	Predict risk for the 20% test set.
2.	Evaluation: Calculate the F1-Score. This ensures you are accurately flagging "Risky" cases while minimizing "False Alarms".
